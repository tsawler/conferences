Checklist

Download PDF

The following checklist will help you see how well your school is doing with waste reduction and give you some new ideas. You may even want to make it your school’s challenge to complete every action on this list.

3R Education

Education is an important part of waste reduction at school. The more that students, teachers, staff and parents know about 3R initiatives, the more likely they are to participate.

  1. Our School has an active environmental group.
  2. Our school incorporates waste reduction issues into course topics.
  3. Our school recognizes the 3R hierarchy-reduce, reuse, then recycle.
  4. Our school plans awareness activities around special occasions like Earth Day, Waste Reduction Week, Compost Awareness Week and Environment Week.
  5. Our school Keeps parents informed of waste reduction activities at school.
  6. Our school encourages all students and teachers to reduce waste at school through PA announcements, posters, memos and other reminders.
  7. Our school shares resources among teachers instead of ordering multiple copies.
  8. Our school has assessed how much waste we generate by conducting waste audit.

Reduce

Reduce comes first in the 3R hierarchy. You can prevent waste from entering our waste disposal systems by reducing at the source- giving the greatest environmental benefit.

  1. Our School holds waste-free lunch days throughout the school year.
  2. Our school encourages students to bring their own reusable cutlery for use in the cafeteria.
  3. Our school makes reusable utensils and plates available at school events where food is served.
  4. Our school always prints and photocopies tests and assignments double-sided
  5. Our school photocopier automatically prints documents double-sided (or our school has posted a sign as a reminder).
  6. Our school communicates via the phone, email and/or school website
  7. Our school monitors the amount of paper that is purchased from one year to the next
  8. Our school avoids purchasing over-packaged material and supplies

Reuse

Reuse is the second R. We can reduce the amount of waste we throw away and eliminate the need to purchase new items by finding new owners and uses for unwanted items.

  1. Our School has a reuse program for school books and supplies
  2. Our school holds used-good sales and/or swap events
  3. Our school collects and reuses paper that is still blank on one side
  4. Our school donates unwanted computers equipment and school furniture
  5. Our school collects and reuses unwanted items for crafts and art projects
  6. Our school has a book exchange in the library for used books and magazines

Recycle

Recycle is the last of the 3 Rs. Recycling lets us turn waste, that could not be eliminated by reducing or reusing, into a resource.

  1. Our School recycles all beverage containers collected at school
  2. Our school has held a recycling blitz
  3. Our school recycles used ink cartridges
  4. Our school recycles paper
  5. Our school recycles some types of electronic waste (i.e. rechargeable batteries, cell phones, CDs)
  6. Our school purchases paper and supplies made from recycled material

Composte

Composting is nature’s way of recycling. We can recycle our organic waste by letting it break down naturally in a compost bin.

  1. Our School is educated about the environment benefits of composting
  2. Our school has educated teachers and students about how to compost
  3. Our school has compost bin set up on school grounds and composts organic waste collected at school
  4. Our school has a vermicompost bin
  5. Our school composts yard waste
  6. Our school uses finished compost on school ground
