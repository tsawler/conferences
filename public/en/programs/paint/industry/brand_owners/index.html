Brand Owners

On May 1, 2008, the government of New Brunswick passed the “Designated Materials Regulation.” It converted the New Brunswick Tire Stewardship Board to a government agency called “Recycle New Brunswick.” Recycle New Brunswick plays an oversight role in the management of specific waste materials, including waste paint.

Recycle NB approved a waste paint management plan and program on April 1, 2009. This program is called an Extended Producer Responsibilityprogram (ERP).  Extended Producer Responsibility means that producers pay directly for the end- of- life waste management of their products.  Thus, the policy removes waste management and its costs from local governments and taxpayers. In addition, the policy spurs product designthat reduces waste disposal costs and recyclingrisks.

All paint brand owners who sell paint in New Brunswick must register with the program.

In the regulation, a “Brand Owner” means a person who

a) manufactures paint in the Province and sells, offers for sale or distributes that paint in the Province,
b) is the owner or licensee in the Province of a registered or unregistered trademark under which the paint is sold, offered for sale or distributed in the Province, or
c) brings paint in to the Province for sale or distribution.
