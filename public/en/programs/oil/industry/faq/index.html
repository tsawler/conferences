FAQ

Whom do I contact if I have questions about the oil and glycol-recycling program?
Please contact Recycle NB at info@recyclenb.com or call the office: 506-454-8473 or
 Toll-free: 1888-322-8473

When will the new oil and glycol recycling program begin?
Recycle NB is working with industry and stakeholders to develop the program. We are targeting January 1, 2014 as the start date for the new recycling program.

What products does the oil and glycol recycling program cover?
The new oil and glycol recycling program covers the following products:

Oil Containers of 50 litres or less
 
Glycol Containers of de 50 litres ou moins
 
Glycol - ethylene or propylene glycol used, or intended for use, as a vehicle or commercial engine coolant. It does not mean plumbing antifreeze; windshield washer antifreeze, lock deicer and gasoline and diesel fuel antifreeze are not included in the regulation. 
 
Oil - petroleum or synthetic derived crankcase oil, engine oil and gear oil, and hydraulic fluid, transmission fluid and heat transfer fluid, and fluid used for lubricating in machinery and equipment.
 
Oil Filter - Any spin-on or element type fluid filter used in hydraulic, transmission or internal combustion engine applications (except  gasoline filters) which includes oil filters, a diesel fuel filters, oil storage tank fuel filters and household furnace oil filters.
 

Brand Owner?
Under the Regulation, a “brand owner” is any person who manufactures, sells, offers for sale, or distributes oil, oil filters, or glycol in the province. A brand owner is also an owner or a licensee in the Province of a registered or unregistered trademark who sells, offers for sale or distributes oil, oil filters and glycol in New Brunswick and any person bringing oil, oil filters or glycol into the Province for use in a commercial enterprise.

I sell oil and glycol products.  Do I have to register with Recycle NB?
If you are a garage, an auto parts dealer or even just a retailer selling oil in your store, you do not have to register with Recycle NB (except as noted above).   Please contact Recycle NB for any regulatory questions.

How do I register as a brand owner for the oil and glycol-recycling program?
To register as a brand owner click here.

Who has to have an oil and glycol recycling program?
All brand owners in the Province must have a management plan. A brand owner is any person who manufactures, sells, offers for sale, or distributes oil, oil filters, or glycol in the province. A brand owner is also an owner or a licensee in the Province of a registered or unregistered trademark who sells, offers for sale or distributes oil, oil filters and glycol in New Brunswick and any person bringing oil, oil filters or glycol into the Province for use in a commercial enterprise.

What happens if the brand owner does not have a stewardship plan?
The Board may refuse to register or suspend the registration of a brand owner who does not submit an oil and glycol stewardship plan.  Failure to comply and maintain a valid registration may require further enforcement action as covered under the Designated Materials Regulation and the Clean Environment Act.

Is a brand owner allowed to appoint a third party to manage its oil and glycol recycling program?
Under Section 37.1, of Regulation 2012-92, a brand owner may designate an agent to manage its recycling program.

Is there an agent in New Brunswick that manages an oil and glycol recycling program for industry?
SOGHUOMA is recognized as the industry agent for the oil and glycol recycling program for all currently registered Brand Owners.

What does an oil and glycol stewardship plan have to cover?
The plan must cover the manufacture, storage, collection, transportation, recycling, disposal or other handling of oil, oil filters, oil containers, glycol and glycol containers. Section 50.16 of Regulation 2012-92 provides the details of the plan. 

Will a brand owner have to set up collection depots for collection of its oil and glycol products?
Yes, a collection system must be set up in rural and urban areas throughout New Brunswick. This will be part of the submitted management plan approved by Recycle NB.

Can a brand owner or retailer pass on the costs connected with an oil and glycol stewardship plan to consumers?
Under the regulation, no retailer, brand owner or wholesaler shall charge a consumer, or show on a consumer’s receipt of sale, a separate fee for the costs of implementing or operating an oil and glycol stewardship plan or supplying educational and consumer material.

Does the Board have to approve a brand owner’s oil and glycol stewardship plan?
As soon as possible after a brand owner submits a stewardship plan, the Board must either approve the plan for a period not to exceed five years or reject the plan in writing with the reasons for the rejection.

What happens if the Board does not approve the brand owner’s stewardship plan?
If the Board rejects a stewardship plan, it may require a brand owner to comply with a plan prepared and approved by the Board or to resubmit a stewardship plan within a specific time.

Is it possible to change a stewardship plan?
The Board may change an approved or imposed plan to correct a clerical error, reflect a change in the name or address of a brand owner, or on the request of a brand owner.
 
A brand owner may apply at any time to have its oil and glycol stewardship plan amended. Sections 50.16 to 50.18 of Regulation 2012-92 apply.


Is there a time limit on a stewardship plan?
A stewardship plan expires on the date set by the Board but no plan shall exceed five years. 90 days before the expiry of a Board approved oil and glycol stewardship plan, the brand owner shall submit a new plan for review and approval.

Does a brand owner have to report on its stewardship plan?
By April 30 of each year, a brand owner must provide the Board with an annual report detailing the effectiveness of the oil and glycol stewardship plan for the previous calendar year.  Section 50.23(1) of Regulation 2012-92 sets out the content for the report. 

Does a brand owner have to provide information regarding its stewardship plan to consumers or retailers?
A brand owner must give retailers information on its plan, return depots, and the benefits of participating in the oil and glycol program.

A brand owner must submit its educational and consumer material to Recycle NB, at least one month in advance of its release.


What should retailers do with the information they receive from brand owners?
Retailers must post or distribute the educational and consumer information. It must be displayed, at the main entrance of the premises and at the area where the transaction to purchase the products takes place.
