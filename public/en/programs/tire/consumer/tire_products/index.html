Recycled Tire Products

Waste tires are more than just garbage or finely ground rubber crumb (steel is removed from the rubber). They are a valuable source of rubber for a range of new and innovative recycledproducts.

Recycled goods made from New Brunswick’s scrap tire resource are well- known for their reliable high quality and durability. They are designed specifically for niche markets in heavy industry, agriculture, construction, and the transport sector.

Tire Recycling Atlantic Canada Corporation (TRACC) in Minto, NB makes all Tire Stewardship Plan products. The TRACC plant’s highest volume output is focused on livestock mattresses for dairy operations across North America and overseas.

Other important products include precision rubber gaskets for use in manufacturing, noise and shock suppression materials, bases for road construction signs. In addition, TRACC has the technical capability to make sophisticated bulk rubber products, such as livestock mattresses, moulded goods, and bark mulch.

Livestock Mattresses

More than 100,000 specially-designed livestock mattresses are made at the TRACC plant every year for installation in advanced dairy production facilities.

Each mattress contains at least 55 kilograms (120 lbs) of industrial rubber recovered from NB scrap tires. Installed with woven coverings stretched over the individual mattresses, they represent a distinct advantage for modern dairy producers.

Jim Walker and his family operate one of Atlantic Canada’s largest dairy farms near Sussex, NB, and use the TRACC ‘Pro-Mat’ system extensively.

“In addition to providing cost-effective shock absorption, anything we can do to prevent bacteria growth in a dairy cow’s immediate surroundings, and diseases like mastitis, makes a lot of sense. These rubber-based mattresses help us do that in several ways,” said Mr. Walker.

National and international demand for these ‘made-in-NB’ products has grown dramatically over the years.

‘Pro-Mat’ livestock mattresses are now routinely shipped from Minto, NB to customers throughout North- America, Europe and beyond.

Moulded Goods

Octagonal rubber bases used to stabilize road repair signposts are among the various molded products manufactured by TRACC from New Brunswick scrap tires.

Known as ‘delineator’ posts, the familiar orange markers with reflecting stripes are designed to block vehicle access from areas being prepared for paving. Such lightweight columns require much heavier ‘bases’ to ensure their stability in different weather conditions, roadbed grades, etc.

Each of the thick rubber disks produced for this purpose by TRACC weighs roughly 7 kilograms (15 lbs) and can easily be doubled-up to provide extra stability at any location. Positive feedback from the road-construction sector over the years underlines their strong market performance.

TRAAC’s diverse range of molded products includes precision-made industrial rubber gaskets, noise or shock suppression mats, and vehicle mud flaps.

Bark Mulch

In addition to molded and fabricated output, new technologies have also opened the way for generating value-added bulk products from NB scrap tires.

One of the most promising applications of this kind involves synthetic rubber ‘bark mulch.’ An advanced formulation of this material developed by TRACC is now prompting widespread interest from the landscaping industry.

The rubber chip output is exceptionally realistic in terms of textures, sizes and shapes. It’s virtually impossible to tell the finished TRACC product from traditional bark mulch used in commercial and residential gardens.

Unlike ‘natural’ bark mulch, however, the recycled rubber equivalent is essentially inert and potential impacts on soil acidity are not a concern.

It’s much higher density also means that it won’t float or wash away easily in heavy rains or under downspouts. The need for repeat applications is substantially lessened as well, and maintenance costs are greatly reduced.
