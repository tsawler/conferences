Waste Reduction

October 20-26 is Waste Reduction Week in Canada.  The annual week-long celebration gives individuals, schools, municipalities and business a chance to lead by example and take action to reduce, reuse and recycle.

As a member of the Waste Reduction Week Council, Recycle NB sponsors the week in New Brunswick. The week is all about is about conveying an appreciation for the richness and beauty of our diverse world and the importance of working toward ecological sustainability by conserving resources and curbing wasteful practices. We hope you will join the waste reduction movement. Visit www.wrwcanada.com website to find out how you can get involved.

Recycle My Cell Challenge

Did you know that old cell phones are valuable and using Recycle My Cell helps keep them out of landfills?

Recycle My Cell, Canada’s national cell phone recycling program, wants to support Waste Reduction Week in Canada (WRW), by making sure that all old and out-of-use cell phones, smartphones, wireless PDA’s, pagers, as well as their batteries and accessories, are properly recycled.

As part of its support and celebration for Waste Reduction Week in Canada 2014, Recycle My Cell  issues a challenge to Canadian schools.  If your school or post-secondary institution is looking for an opportunity to make a difference environmentally then this is for you!  Sign up for the challenge at www.wrwcanada.ca

Canadian students from kindergarten to the post-secondary level are challenged to recycle as many old wireless devices and accessories as possible between October 20 and November 21, 2014.  Last year’s New Brunswick winner was the Donald Fraser Memorial School in Plaster Rock.

Ease My Load Campaign

According to the latest figures, Canadians are creating 1031 kg of waste per person each year. Over 75% of this waste is just piling up in landfills all over the country. Want to do something to change that? Join the Ease My Load Campaign and share your actions with Canadians.

Waste Tire Recycling

Recycling waste tires helps protect the environment and creates new rubber products and jobs in New Brunswick. Help maintain our clean safe, environment and celebrate Waste Reduction Week by recycling your waste tires.  

Under Pressure

Maintaining the recommended tire pressure for your vehicle’s tires is a smart move! Low tire pressure threatens the safety of you and your passengers, contributes to global warming, and increases your fuel bill. Be Tire Smart! Measure your tire pressure monthly. 

Waste Reduction Week in Canada is October 20-26, 2014. Why don’t you start your monthly tire pressure check during this week? Its one small step you can take to protect the environment and your driving safety.

Don’t know what the recommended tire pressure is for your vehicle? Not sure how to measure your tire pressure?  For more information on tire safety go to www.betiresmart.ca/

Waste Paint Recycling

Recycling your waste or unwantedpaint is one way you can help to protect our clean, safe environment. Recycling waste paint is easy in New Brunswick; just drop it off at the nearest paint-recycling depot in your area. Go to www.productcare.org to find the one nearest you. Just take your paint and the recycling depot will do the rest.  For more information, check out the New Brunswick waste paint management program.

Oil and Glycol Recycling

It is easy for you to recycle used oil, oil filters, oil containers, glycol and glycol containers in New Brunswick. Recycle NB oversees an industry- led, and financed, oil and glycol management plan. SOGHUOMA manages the plan on behalf of industry. It covers the manufacture, storage, collection, transportation, recycling and disposal of these products. Click here for recycling locations.
