October 21, 2014

Waste Reduction Week

Fredericton- October 20-26 is Waste Reduction Week in Canada and Recycle NB is encouraging New Brunswickers to make recycling a part of their regular routine.

“As the sponsor of Waste Reduction Week in New Brunswick, we hope that everyone will use this week to think about how they can reduce, reuse and recycle,” says Recycle NB Chair Bryan Howell. “We want every week to be waste reduction week in New Brunswick.”

Howell points to the recycling of old tires as one way to help protect the environment and says it is easy to do in New Brunswick. “Just drop off waste tires at any tire retailer in the province and they will be recycled into new rubber products at the Tire Recycling Atlantic Canada plant in Minto.”

New  Brunswick has had a waste management program for tires for over 16 years. The program protects the environment by diverting waste tires from landfills and turning them into recycled rubber products. Since it began, the program has diverted one million tires per year from the province’s landfills.

Along with tires, Recycle NB also manages the recycling of waste paint in the province. The paint management program has been in place for five years and, according to CEO Pat McCarthy, it has more than exceeded expectations. 72 % of the paint collected is turned into new paint products.  McCarthy credits New Brunswickers with the success of the program.

In 2014 Recycle NB, in partnership with industry, implemented a used oil and glycol stewardship program for New Brunswick.“ Theimplementation of this program is another step in making sure that all waste materials in New Brunswick are managed in a sustainable and environmentally responsible way,” said Howell. “Recycling these products will contribute to the sustainability of our environment.”

McCarthy adds his organization receives calls from the public asking about the recycling of electronics. Recycle NB is looking forward to putting a waste management plan in place for these products in the near future. “Our programs are a success because people across the province make an effort to recycle.  We hope they will continue to reduce, reuse and recycle,” he said.

Information on Recycle NB and its programs is available online at www.recyclenb.com
